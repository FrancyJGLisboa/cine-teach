03 / 06
Yinsen's dying words: 'Don't waste your life'
System prompt

Tony didn't choose Yinsen's last words, but every choice he makes afterward answers to them.

Where it breaksTony carries the words as memory; the real instructions are re-pasted in front of the AI before every single turn.

A system prompt is standing text the operator prepends to every request; the model reads it fresh each time and cannot rewrite it.

Subagents

Tony hands Pepper one job — she does it her own way, with what she alone knows.

Where it breaksIn the film her findings flow to the agents, not to Tony; a real delegate reports back to its sender, then vanishes.

Real delegates are fresh AI instances: one assigned task, a private working conversation, and a single summary sent back to the requester.

05 / 06

Permission gates

The new armor stays a picture on the screen until Tony says build it.

Where it breaksIn the film the pause is JARVIS's manner; in reality it is enforced machinery the AI cannot talk its way past.

Before risky actions — deleting files, sending money — the harness freezes and a human must click approve.

Hooks

Dum-E waits with the extinguisher and sprays on his own the instant something looks like fire.

Where it breaksDum-E can only douse a fire after it starts; a real check can also veto the action before it ever runs.

Hooks are small scripts the user registers; the harness runs them automatically around matching actions and they can cancel the action outright.

Where the analogy ends

Context compaction

When the conversation grows past what the AI can read at once, the software replaces the oldest exchanges with a short summary — detail is deliberately lost so the gist can keep fitting.

These ideas are genuinely new — the movie has no shape for them.
