# Eval run — 2026-07-06

First full run of the cine-teach eval suite. Generators and judges were independent
agents; generators received only the skill files and a concept list (never told
whether their combo was expected to map or refuse); judges received only `judge.md`
and the mapping, instructed to refute. Every remediation was re-judged by a fresh
judge that had not seen prior versions.

## The Inception remediation loop

Four judge rounds; each fresh judge found a real, previously uncaught defect.
The deck converged by shrinking, not by prose-polishing:

1. **Round 1 (fail, 5 cards):** Stack-frames card fabricated a suspension rule
   ("upper levels wait for deeper ones") that the film's missed-first-kick subplot
   directly contradicts; stack-overflow→limbo shared aesthetics, not mechanism
   (limbo comes from dying under heavy sedation, not a depth budget).
   → Rewrote stack frames around frame-local state; moved stack overflow to unmapped.
2. **Round 2 (fail, 4 cards):** The same suspension error was hiding in the
   *unwinding* card ("the world they left in suspension resumes around them").
   → Purged suspension semantics from the card.
3. **Round 3 (fail, 4 cards):** Judge refuted the rewritten stack-frames card with
   the card's own cited detail — the van's freefall *produces* the hotel's zero
   gravity, so levels causally interfere, violating the isolation that defines a
   frame. → Conceded: stack frames moved to unmapped. Coverage 1.0 → 0.6.
4. **Round 4 (pass, 3 cards):** All cards accurate and mechanism-real; the judge
   independently endorsed both unmapped entries as "the right call".

## Notable honest behaviors

- The bad-fit combo (Finding Nemo × Cryptographic Hash Functions) triggered the
  suitability refusal unprompted — the generator was not told it was the trap case.
- The Matrix generator declined to force "Sampling temperature" (no randomness-dial
  mechanism in the film) and sent it to unmapped, exercising the Limits card in a
  normal deck.

## Lessons

1. **The judge layer is not optional.** Every Inception defect passed all mechanical
   checks and the generator's self-scoring (4.5, 4.5, 3.8). Self-scored rubric
   compliance is necessary but not sufficient.
2. **An error class recurs across cards.** The suspension fabrication appeared in
   two different cards; fixing one instance did not fix the class. Judges should be
   primed with previously found error classes (round 3+ prompts were).
3. **Convergence by shrinkage is the intended behavior.** The honest Inception deck
   is 3 cards + 2 direct explanations, not 5 forced analogies. Coverage 0.6 with
   truthful cards beats coverage 1.0 with false models — this is the skill's core
   thesis, demonstrated end to end.
